Togro , Wireframes & UX System

Mobile-first wireframes and end-to-end UX for the four Togro roles. This revision adds a dedicated photo-verification flow and replaces emoji with a custom line-icon set, scan-line, pulse, and ring animations.

1 · Design language

Togro should feel calm, confident, and tactile , closer to Strava and Airbnb than to a council app. Map-first, icon-led, single-handed outdoor use.

Citrus Lime, primary brand Field Clay, surfaces Charcoal, ink Slate, secondary Hazard Amber, alerts Sky Blue, info & water Bone, background
  • Type: Inter / SF Pro. Display weight only on numbers and headings; everything else 15–17px regular.
  • Icons: Custom 24px line set (2px stroke) , no emoji. Hazards, livestock, routes, rewards all share the same drawing language.
  • Motion: Pulse rings on the FAB and current location, scan-line on photo verification, ring-bursts on credit earn, spring transitions (200–300ms).
  • Voice: Short, plainspoken, never bureaucratic. "Path closed ahead" not "Notification of temporary access restriction".

3 · Photo verification , how it works

A trustworthy hazard report is the foundation of the credits economy. The verification system runs invisibly in seconds and combines four signals.

SignalWhat it checksWhy
GPS & motionPhone is physically at the hazard, not spoofed; accelerometer matches walkingStops armchair reporters
Photo freshnessEXIF timestamp within 60s, no screenshots, no re-uploadsStops recycled images
On-device MLDetects: cattle, sheep, fallen tree, fence, fire, blocked path, dogAuto-classifies & rejects blanks
Cross-reportSecond independent walker within 24h on same pinAuto-promotes to Verified
User trust score0–100, decays with rejected reportsTrusted users skip the queue

States a report can be in

  • Pending , visible only to the reporter, no credits yet.
  • Auto-verified , ML + cross-report agree → live on the map, credits awarded.
  • Needs review , single source, low ML confidence, or low-trust user → admin queue.
  • Cleared , another walker confirms it's gone → original reporter earns +1.
  • Rejected , admin or ML flagged spam → −2 credits & trust hit.
Why this UX matters
Walkers see the verification process happening in front of them , scan-line, ticking checks, ring burst on success. It feels transparent and rewarding, not like a black box. They learn what makes a "good" report just by watching it work.
Privacy guardrails
Faces and number plates are blurred on-device before upload. Photos containing only people are auto-rejected. EXIF GPS is stripped from publicly displayed images.

How the plugin model works

  • Connect once via OAuth (or API key / CSV fallback). Farmer chooses exactly which data flows in.
  • Suggested, not automatic. Events land in an inbox. One tap to publish, edit, or dismiss. After repeated patterns, farmer can flip specific event types to auto-publish.
  • Two-way sync. Walker reports on the farmer's land (open gate, injured sheep, fly-tipping) push back into the connected tool as a task or note.
  • Land model. Field parcels imported once from RPA/SBI or KML so alerts attach to a real field, not a dropped pin.
  • Audit trail. Every published alert is timestamped and signed , useful for NFU / insurance after an incident.
Launch partners to target
Herdwatch, Breedr, AgriWebb, FieldMargin, Farmplan/Gatekeeper, Agrimetrics, BCMS / ScotEID / WLBP, Google & Outlook Calendar, WhatsApp (one-way bridge). A small public webhook spec lets any UK ag-tech vendor build their own Togro connector.

How the sanitiser works

  • Strip identifiers. Animal IDs, ear tags, breed, exact counts, valuations, owner names are never sent to the public layer.
  • Fuzz the location. Default 200m radius around the real point. Farmer can tighten or loosen per farm.
  • Exclude sensitive zones. Farmhouse, equipment store, fuel tanks and high-value paddocks are drawn once and permanently masked from the public map.
  • Translate to walker language. "Holstein heifers in calf, paddock 7" becomes "Cows with calves ahead, keep dogs on leads."
  • Quiet hours. Nothing publishes overnight, so a published alert never doubles as a "no one is watching" signal.
  • Per-alert preview. Farmer always sees the public version before it goes live, with a one-tap "this is too much, hide".
  • Audit log. Every published alert and every redacted field is timestamped, signed, and exportable for NFU Mutual or police.
Why this matters
Rural crime is a real cost, livestock theft, machinery theft, hare coursing. A naive hazard map could accidentally tell a thief exactly where the valuable cows are tonight. Togro's job is to give walkers enough to stay safe and respectful, while giving farmers a guarantee that their operational data stays operational.
Reconfirmation also uses the camera
The "Still here / Not there" prompt now requires a quick photo. Same verification pipeline as a fresh report, same +1 credit, same trust score impact. No photo means no credit, which keeps drive-by tapping out of the loop.

7 · End-to-end UX flows

Walker , earn & redeem loop

  1. Open app → map home (location, nearby hazards, credits visible top-right).
  2. Tap pulsing camera FAB → camera opens instantly, GPS captured silently.
  3. Tap a hazard chip (livestock / path / fire / other), submit.
  4. Verification screen runs scan-line + live checks: location, freshness, ML, cross-report.
  5. Ring-burst success animation: "+2 credits, Trusted Reporter".
  6. Wallet shows balance growing; nearest reachable reward surfaces.
  7. Tap reward → confirm cost → QR + 5-min countdown → staff scans → success.

Farmer , alert flow

  1. Open app → "My land" with active alerts.
  2. Add alert → pick icon → tap map area → choose duration → publish.
  3. Walkers within 5 km routing through that area get a re-route prompt.
  4. Alert auto-expires; farmer gets a one-tap "Still active?" reminder.

Business , claim & reward flow

  1. Search address → "Claim this business" → email/phone verify.
  2. Create first reward in <30s using a template (coffee / discount / bundle).
  3. Reward goes live on the walker map; ROI dashboard tracks footfall.
  4. Staff opens "Scan" tab at the till to redeem walker QR codes.

Admin , moderation flow

  1. Queue auto-prioritised: low-trust users + single-source reports first.
  2. Keyboard shortcuts: A approve · R reject · F flag · B ban reporter.
  3. Bulk-select duplicates within 50 m / 30 min for one-click merge.
  4. Every action logged with reason; reversible for 24 h.
Interaction principles
  • Camera and report are always one tap from anywhere.
  • Pulse rings on the FAB and current location signal "live".
  • Scan-line animation during photo verification builds trust.
  • Ring-burst on credit earn , a small physical celebration.
  • Offline: queued reports sync when signal returns; wallet works offline.
  • Outdoor mode auto-engages in bright sun (high contrast, larger taps).

7b · Hazard lifecycle, decay & reconfirmation

A hazard is not a permanent pin. Every report has a half-life. The map only shows fresh, trusted information, and walkers earn small credits for keeping it that way.

Decay model

  • Each hazard type has a default time-to-live: livestock 48h, fallen tree 14d, path closure 7d, fire risk 24h, loose dog 6h.
  • Confidence decays linearly from 100% on day zero to 0% at TTL. Pin opacity on the map fades in step.
  • At 50% confidence the system asks the next walker who routes within 100m: "Is this still here?" One tap: Still here, Gone, Not sure.
  • "Still here" resets confidence to 100% and extends TTL. The original reporter gets +1, the confirmer gets +1.
  • "Gone" drops confidence to zero, archives the pin, and credits the confirmer +1 for clearing it.
  • No confirmation by TTL: pin auto-archives silently, no penalty to the original reporter.

Who can report, and how credits work

RoleCan report?Earns credits?Why
WalkerYes, anywhereYesCore contributor loop
FarmerYes, on owned/connected landNo, but earns trust score and insurance audit logThey already benefit, credits would be a conflict of interest
BusinessYes, within a defined radius of their venueNo, but reports auto-trustedLocal eyes, same logic as farmers
Council / rangerYes, anywhere in their patchNo, official sourceReports skip the queue and auto-verify

Farmer and business reports are marked with a small "verified source" badge on the walker map so users see they came from the landowner directly.

Access type, every pin and route knows it

  • Public right of way, footpath, bridleway, byway, restricted byway. Default open access.
  • Permissive path, landowner allows access at their discretion. Can be revoked or scheduled (e.g. closed during lambing).
  • CROW open access land, mapped under the Countryside and Rights of Way Act.
  • Private with consent, walker must have a token from the landowner (e.g. shoot syndicate, fishing beat).
  • No access, drawn for routing only, never suggested to walkers.

Farmers can flip a permissive path to "closed today" with one tap, and the routing engine instantly re-routes anyone heading that way. Walkers see a soft amber banner: "This permissive path is closed by the landowner until tomorrow, thanks for respecting it."

Why decay matters
Stale hazards are worse than no hazards, walkers stop trusting the map. Decay plus reconfirmation keeps the data fresh without anyone needing to babysit it, and gives walkers a second, lower-friction way to earn credits beyond first-time reporting.

Anti-abuse logic

  • Trust score per user (0–100). Drives queue priority and reward eligibility.
  • GPS sanity: phone must physically be at the hazard location; speed and accelerometer cross-checked.
  • Photo ML: blur, duplicate, screenshot, indoor scene detection.
  • Cross-verification window: single report = "pending"; two independent reports within 24h auto-verify.
  • Daily earnable cap (e.g. 12/day) to prevent farming.
  • Per-location cool-down: same hazard from same user within 2h ignored.
  • Redemption fraud: rotating QR codes, single-use, server-validated, business-scanner pairing.

Business ROI guarantees

  • Footfall attribution: dashboard shows redemptions, est. spend lift, repeat visits.
  • Simple economics: business sets the credit cost , Togro never gives away their margin.
  • Reward caps: per-day limits prevent oversubscription.
  • Quiet-day boost: businesses can 2× credits on Tuesdays to drive demand when they want it.
  • Monthly summary email: three numbers , redemptions, new visitors, repeat visitors.

9 · Engagement & retention loops

  • Streaks: "5-day walker" badge, gentle nudge if a streak is about to break.
  • Local leaderboards: "Top contributors near Edale this week" , pride, not pressure.
  • Seasonal challenges: "Spring lambing watch , log 5 livestock alerts, earn a free pint."
  • Surprise drops: occasional "Mystery reward unlocked" push to keep the loop fresh.
  • Community trust badges: Trusted Reporter, Local Guide, Path Guardian.
  • Walker ↔ farmer thank-yous: farmers can send a one-tap "Thanks for the heads-up" + 1 credit tip.
  • Re-engagement: if you walk past an unredeemed reward, a soft notification fires once.
